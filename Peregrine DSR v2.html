section 02  /  business case

The problem you're solving.

Three reasons Peregrine is evaluating a change now — and why waiting compounds the cost.

01

Procurement that was set up for the wrong business.

A platform designed for global manufacturers was deployed to a complex, mixed-brand portfolio — full configuration, no accommodation for how select-service hotel operations actually work. The result: friction at every property level and low adoption across the portfolio.

02

The procure-to-pay loop was never fully closed.

The goal was a clean flow: PO submitted, invoice matched, coding applied, data into the accounting system. Without native inventory, without intelligent invoice coding, without a checkbook tied to your operating budget, that loop stays open — and your team spends time filling the gaps manually.

03

Visibility at the corporate level is limited.

Without a direct integration pushing live PO spend data into Profit Sword, GMs can't see where they stand against budget until it's too late to act. Month-end becomes a surprise review instead of a confirmation of what the team already managed in real time.

section 03  /  proposed solution

What Reeco brings to Peregrine.

Hospitality-native from day one. Built for the way mixed-brand portfolios actually run.

01
Built for hotels. Only for hotels.
Reeco is purpose-built for hospitality operations — not adapted from a manufacturing or retail procurement system. One platform covers purchasing, AP automation, inventory, and culinary management. Property staff interact with an item-first marketplace: browse, compare prices across suppliers side-by-side, submit a PO. No punch-out, no department dropdown menus.
02
PO approvals tied directly to your Profit Sword forecast.
Before a PO is approved, Reeco shows the approver exactly where that spend lands against the current GL budget pulled from Profit Sword. GMs see the variance in real time and make the call before the order goes out — not after month-end close. Intacct integration is direct: once credentials are configured, data flows automatically with no manual mapping.
03
AP automation purpose-built for Sage Intacct.
Reeco's invoice processing is purpose-built for hospitality finance teams running Intacct — not retrofitted. Invoices are captured, AI-coded by line item and supplier, matched against the PO, and queued for Intacct sync. The coding learns over time. This is a fundamentally different experience than what enterprise procurement platforms offer for hospitality back-office workflows.

section 10  /  faqs

Questions we expect — answered directly.

If something's missing, email Zander and we'll add it before June 3.

"We already have a procurement platform in place. Why make a change?"
The question isn't whether your current platform works in theory — it's whether it works for your team in practice. Reeco is enterprise-grade software, purpose-built exclusively for hotels. The difference shows up in day-to-day use: property staff can place orders without training sessions, invoices code themselves, and your corporate team sees real spend data in Profit Sword without manual intervention.
"How hard is the implementation? We've been through a difficult rollout before."
Reeco does not require a dedicated full-time resource to manage the implementation. We'll present a full implementation plan specific to your portfolio at the June 3 meeting, including integration sequencing, property rollout phasing, and timeline. Sage Intacct is Reeco's fastest and most direct integration path.
"Will the Intacct and Profit Sword integrations actually work for our setup?"
Yes. Sage Intacct integration is direct — once a Web Services user is configured with the correct role permissions, data flows automatically with no manual mapping. Profit Sword receives daily PO spend from Reeco and sends budget and GL data back, giving GMs a live checkbook view tied to the current forecast.
"What about adoption at the property level?"
Reeco is designed for the user who isn't a procurement professional. It looks and behaves like a shopping experience — search for an item, see prices from all your suppliers side-by-side, submit a PO. No complex menus, no punch-out to supplier sites, no coding required at the property level. The AP automation handles the back-end.
