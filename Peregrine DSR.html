section 02  /  business case

The problem you're solving.

Three reasons Peregrine is evaluating a change now — and why waiting compounds the cost.

01

Coupa was built for Nike, not hotels.

A full Coupa implementation pushed to select‑service GMs who face 150-department dropdown menus to code a single cleaning invoice. Procurement that was supposed to streamline operations created friction at every property level.

02

The CFO has a balance sheet problem.

$1.5M sunk into a platform that isn't delivering. Every month Coupa stays, the write-down gets harder. The question isn't whether to move — it's how to structure the move to absorb the cost cleanly.

03

Missing the core functions that matter.

No treasury checkbook tied to Profit Sword, no native inventory, no line-item invoice intelligence, no procurement-native payment processing. The procure-to-pay loop was never fully closed.

section 03  /  proposed solution

How Reeco solves it.

Hospitality-native from day one. Built for the way 47-property portfolios actually run.

01
Hospitality-native from day one.
Reeco is purpose-built for hotels — purchasing, receiving, AP automation, inventory, and recipe costing in one platform. One login. One system. No punch-out, no supplier-site bouncing, no department dropdowns. A select-service GM can place an order in under a minute.
02
The Intacct and Profit Sword integration is the fastest in the business.
Reeco connects directly to Sage Intacct with zero manual mapping — once a Web Services user is created with correct role permissions, data flows automatically. Profit Sword receives daily PO spend data from Reeco, and budget/GL data flows back in. GMs see spend vs. budget in real time and self‑correct before month-end — no more variance surprises on financial statements.
03
Invoice processing goes from 7–10 minutes to ~60 seconds.
Reeco's AI reads line-item detail, codes by SKU and supplier, and learns over time — exactly the intelligent coding behavior Craftable had, modernized. For a portfolio generating $220M in F&B annually, that's roughly 800 hours per property per year returned to your team.

section 10  /  faqs & objections

Questions we expect — answered directly.

If something's missing, email Zander and we'll add it before June 3.

"We already have $1.5M sunk into Coupa. Why would we absorb that loss?"
You don't have to absorb it all at once. A multi-year contract structure with Reeco can be built specifically to spread the Coupa write-down over time, converting a one-time balance sheet hit into a manageable amortized exit. Paul Bennie will walk through the scenarios on June 3.
"How hard is implementation? Coupa took a dedicated full-time person."
Reeco does not. Coupa is a complex enterprise procurement suite designed for global manufacturers — implementation requires configuration, custom cXML punch-out connections, and extensive change management. Reeco is hospitality-native and deploys in weeks, not months. We'll bring a full 47-property implementation plan to the June 3 meeting with a clear timeline and resource model.
"Will your Intacct and Profit Sword integrations actually work for our setup?"
Yes — Sage Intacct is Reeco's fastest integration path. Once a Web Services user is created with the correct role permissions, data flows automatically — no manual mapping, no custom development. Profit Sword integration pushes daily PO data and receives GL/budget data back. We've done this before and we'll walk you through it on June 3.
"What about our select-service properties? Coupa was a disaster for those GMs."
This is exactly the use case Reeco was built for. No punch-out, no 150-department dropdowns. A select-service GM can log in, see their suppliers, compare prices, and place an order in under a minute. The UI is item-first — you browse like you're shopping, not configuring an ERP.
